A Tour of Webster University

Sverdrup Business/Technology Complex
The Leif J. Sverdrup Business/Technology Complexis home to the George Herbert Walker School of Business & Technology and the School of Communications. The facility consists of three contiguous buildings that house classrooms, faculty offices and student computer labs.

Webster Hall
Webster HallBuilt in 1915, Webster Hall was the first building constructed on campus. Today, historic Webster Hall still stands as the administrative headquarters of a vast network that now includes campus locations in more than 20 states, as well as international campuses in Europe and Asia

The Emerson Library
A campus centerpiece, the state-of-the-artEmerson Library features a 24-hour cyber cafe, private study rooms, an electronic classroom, and online workstations connected to a wide variety of databases. Oh, and books: more than 200,000 volumes of monographs, rare books, periodicals, videos and other media. All staffed by librarians who help you find the right resources to ace any assignment.

Loretto-Hilton Center for Performing Arts
The Loretto-Hilton Center for Performing Arts is one of Webster's primary links to the St. Louis arts community. In addition to serving as a training ground for students in Webster's Leigh Gerdine College of Fine Arts, the facility is home to The Repertory Theatre of St. Louis and Opera Theatre of St. Louis.
